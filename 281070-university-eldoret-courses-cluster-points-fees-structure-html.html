University of Eldoret courses, cluster points &amp; fees structure

The University of Eldoret is an institution of higher learning located nine kilometers from Eldoret town in Uasin Gishu county, Kenya. The university was founded in 1946 as a Large-scale farming Training Center for white settlers and was converted into a teacher’s training college in 1984 under the name Moi Teachers Training College. The college was taken over by the Moi University in 1990 due to the double intake crisis and named the Chepkoilel Campus. In February, 2013, the institution was granted a charter by the president and became the University of Eldoret. Ever since, the University of Eldoret courses and programs have expanded to meet student needs and workforce demand in Kenya. Initially, the university had the school of forestry and science only but has since grown and now has nine schools with thirty-four academic departments.

University of Eldoret bridging courses

The University of Eldoret offers bridging courses in Mathematics at its Eldoret Town campus. The classes are offered full time and cost twenty one thousand two hundred Kenyan shillings. the minimum entry requirements for the program include a mean grade of C+ and above in the KCSE.

Certificate courses at the University of Eldoret include the Certificate in Environmental Impact Assessment (EIA). The certificate is one of the courses offered at the Eldoret town campus of the university.

Fee structure

A guide on the fee structures for the various programs is available on the University's website. According to the 2015/2016 academic year fee structure, the costs for pursuing a degree course at UoE ranges between one hundred thousand Kenyan shillings to one hundred and fifty thousand Kenyan shillings per semester for PSSP students depending on the course, plus other costs such as the fees for field attachment and field trips. The same year, tuition fees for governemnts sponsored students was Ksh. 16000.

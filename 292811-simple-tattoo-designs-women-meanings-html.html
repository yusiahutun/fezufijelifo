Tattoo designs for women's back

They say go big or go home! Tattoos put on the back are usually big. This is because the back offers so much flat space unlike the stomach which is all bumpy. Here are some popular tattoo designs for the back.

1. Phoenix tattoo

This is a tattoo of the phoenix bird. This tattoo has a very deep meaning. It means that she has risen from the flames as a winner, beating all life challenges and defeating hard times. The phoenix is a symbol of rebirth from the ashes of the past, and it also represents the victory of life over death, thus immortality.

2. Beauty from within

We all need to meditate. Once in awhile, it is nice to listen to your soul. This tattoo symbolizes balance. Balance of the inside and the outside. That is why there are hills and mountains inside her body. It also represents oneness. Oneness with self and the surrounding. Isn't this a good way to be in touch with mother nature?

3. Inspirational back tattoo

This is an inspiration tattoo. There are many people who tattoo themselves different phrases. We all have phrases that we live by and this is a sure way of making sure you never forget what you live by. This tattoo says 'I am the architect of my own destruction'. This means if she fails, she is to blame for it. It is being accountable to yourself. If she also succeeds it is because of herself. This woman has clearly taken control of her life. Are you in control of your life or are you still blaming people for your doings?

4. Yin-Yang tattoo

This tattoo originated from Asia. It has a couple of meanings to it. It is usually a circle with two 'halves' for lack of a better word to describe the two segments. One half is black with a small white circle and the other half is white with a small black circle. This tattoo first of all means balance as there is equal black and white in it. It also means the balance in the good (white) and evil(black). The Yin-Yang also means that there is good in the bad and there is bad in the good, that is why there is a white circle in the black segment and vice versa.

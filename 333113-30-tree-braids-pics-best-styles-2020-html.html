How long do tree braids last?

When you get yours styled by a professional and give them proper care and maintenance, they might last for up to three months. Ideally, most people go for two months, which is quite manageable. Since they are a solution for brittle hair, you can have it in for as long as you desire, but keep it within the threshold.

How much does tree braids cost?

Tree braids are very common as they offer a stunning look. The cost of getting this style depends on the complexity of the hairstyle you chose. Some prefer doing it at home where they can get a friend who can charge them a few coins to get the preferred look. Another option is going to a professional hairdresser. Do not forget about the extension cost.

Are tree braids and crochet braids the same?

It is hard to spot the difference between tree braids and crotchet braids since both of them have the same foundation â€“ cornrows. The main distinction between these two styles is how the extension hair is added.

When doing tree braids, the hairdresser starts with cornrows and then adds hair. The hair is then pulled out as they braid. Contrary to this, during the crocheting process, the hairdresser goes back with a crotchet to hook the hair.

Do braids help grow hair?

Not really. The main idea behind braiding your hair is to protect and allow it to rest and rejuvenate. However, since the braids pull your hair roots, it may promote root activity. It is important to remember that hair growth is purely genetic. Although braiding creates conducive conditions for hair growth, it does not change your destiny.

Why do my braids itch?

Many individuals that choose to add braids are aware of the itchiness that follows. The main reason behind this is the chemical utilized to coat synthetic hair. Although the itchiness is irritating, there are very many strategies for dealing with it. You can use hair products that counter this effect or go for natural remedies.

How long should I keep my braids in?

Braids are considered a protective style for natural hair. Therefore, it suffices that the less disturbance, the better for your hair health. Be careful not to keep your braids in more extended than the usual time of between four to eight weeks, as you might experience hair breakage.

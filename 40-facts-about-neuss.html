Neuss is one of the oldest cities in Germany.

With a history that dates back over 2,000 years, Neuss has witnessed the rise and fall of empires, making it an archaeological treasure trove.

The city is situated on the west bank of the Rhine River.

This prime location provides stunning views of the river and allows visitors to enjoy scenic walks along the waterfront.

Neuss is known for its impressive Roman heritage.

The city was founded by the Romans in 16 BC, and remnants of their presence can be seen in the many archaeological sites and museums.

The famous Roman military commander, Julius Caesar, used Neuss as a base during his campaign against the Germanic tribes.

This historical connection adds to the city’s allure and attracts history enthusiasts from around the world.

Neuss is home to the iconic Quirinus Münster.

This impressive Romanesque basilica is dedicated to Saint Quirinus and is considered one of the most important religious buildings in Germany.

The Neusser Bürger-Schützenfest is the largest volunteer festival in the world.

Every year, the city comes alive with parades, shooting competitions, and vibrant celebrations that attract thousands of participants and spectators.

Neuss is famous for its mustard.

The city’s traditional mustard recipe has been passed down for generations, making it a popular local delicacy.

The city is a hub for business and industry.

Neuss boasts a thriving economy, with companies from various sectors contributing to its growth and prosperity.

Neuss is known for its vibrant arts and cultural scene.

The city hosts numerous festivals, exhibitions, and concerts throughout the year, showcasing its rich cultural heritage.

Neuss is the birthplace of the modern circus.

In 1768, Philip Astley, a former cavalry officer, established the world’s first modern circus in Neuss, introducing the concept of ring-based entertainment.

The impressive Augustinus Fountain is a popular landmark in Neuss.

Located in the city center, this fountain depicts various scenes from Neuss’ history and is a favorite spot for locals and tourists alike.

Neuss is home to the Clemens Sels Museum.

This renowned art museum houses an extensive collection of paintings, sculptures, and decorative arts spanning different periods.

The city’s proximity to Düsseldorf offers easy access to a range of shopping and entertainment options.

Neuss is just a short distance away from one of Germany’s most vibrant and dynamic cities, adding to its appeal as a tourist destination.

The Neusser Racecourse is a popular destination for horse racing enthusiasts.

The racecourse hosts exciting horse racing events throughout the year, attracting both locals and visitors.

Neuss is known for its picturesque parks and green spaces.

From the idyllic Westpark to the tranquil Rheinpark, the city offers plenty of opportunities to enjoy nature and relax.

The city is renowned for its annual Christmas market.

During the festive season, the streets of Neuss come alive with charming stalls selling crafts, food, and mulled wine.

Neuss is home to the International Dance Weeks.

This renowned dance festival brings together talented dancers and choreographers from around the world for captivating performances.

The city hosts the famous Rheinland Carnival.

During this lively celebration, locals don colorful costumes, sing traditional songs, and participate in parades and street parties.

Neuss is a paradise for food enthusiasts.

The city offers a wide variety of culinary delights, including local specialties, international cuisines, and fine dining experiences.

Neuss has a rich sports culture.

From football and basketball to swimming and archery, the city offers numerous sports facilities and opportunities for active individuals.

The city’s Neue Rennbahn is the ideal venue for horse racing fans.

This modern racetrack hosts thrilling races and offers a unique entertainment experience.

FAQs

Q: What is the best time to visit Neuss?

A: The best time to visit Neuss is during the summer months of June, July, and August when the weather is pleasant and many festivals and events take place.

Q: How can I get to Neuss?

A: Neuss is easily accessible by both train and car. It is located just a short distance from Düsseldorf, making it convenient for travelers coming from the surrounding regions.

Q: What are some popular attractions in Neuss?

A: Some popular attractions in Neuss include the Neuss Abbey, the Quirinus Münster, and the Clemens-Sels-Museum. The city also boasts beautiful parks and gardens.

Q: What traditional dishes can I try in Neuss?

A: Some traditional dishes you can try in Neuss include Rheinischer Sauerbraten (marinated pot roast), Rheinischer Döppekuchen (potato dish), and Neusser Mostert (mustard).

Q: Are there any hotels in Neuss?

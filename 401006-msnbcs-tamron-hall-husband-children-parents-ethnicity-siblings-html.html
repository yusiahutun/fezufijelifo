MSNBC's Tamron Hall: husband, children, parents, ethnicity, siblings

Tamron Hall is an award-winning broadcast journalist, television talk host, and a talented executive producer. The Texas-born media personality has worked with some of the biggest media houses such as MSNBC and NBC, both as a daytime anchor and TV host. Currently, she hosts Tamron Hall Show.

Besides being one of the most celebrated media personalities, working in different media houses has created a buzz around her name. Speculations about the cause of her movements in different media outlets, for example, have made her one of the most talked-about TV hosts in the USA.

Early life, family, education, and ethnicity

Tamron Hall was born on September 16, 1970, in Texas. Although her mother was a single parent, she gave Tamron and her sister the best life. When her mother remarried, the broadcaster and her sister quickly adapted to the new life. However, Tamron Hall’s parents did not move out of Texas.

She attended different Luling schools in Texas before going to Pennsylvania for college. In 1992, the talented journalist graduated from Temple University with a Bachelor of Arts in Broadcast Journalism. She shortly worked at Dallas-Fort as a fresh graduate before relocating to Bryan in Texas.

What ethnicity is Tamron Hall? The TV star is an Afro-American. Apart from being open about her ethnicity in her shows, Tamron is part of the National Association of Black Journalists. As a member of the association, she is vocal on black stories, especially in the entertainment scene.

Is Tamron Hall related to Arsenio Hall? While the two talk show hosts share the last name, they are not related. Arsenio is from Cleveland, Ohio, while Tamron is from Texas. Besides being from different states, they have never pointed in any of their interviews that they are related.

Work and career highlights

After graduating from college in 1992, Tamron worked in different media houses in different capacities. Some of her career highlights in the past three decades include:

As a reporter, anchor, and TV host (on MSNBC and NBC News)

From 2007 to 2017, Tamron Hall was one of the most influential media personalities on MSNBC and NBC. She worked as a News reporter and fill-in anchor for different shows. Also, her interviews before the 2008 elections were unmatched.

In 2008, Tamron interviewed Barack Obama. While the then-presidential aspirant had a ground in the Democratic Party, the interview changed the national perception of what Obama stood for, as a leader.

Thanks to her unrivalled interviewing skills, Tamron started NewsNation. In her show, she conducted high-profile interviews that redefined global and national politics in the USA. She also reported the news. For example, when Osama Bin Laden was killed, she was one of the broadcasters that broke the news to the world.

As the Today show co-anchor

After an impressive year in 2013, Tamron joined the Today show as a co-host. She became the first woman of colour to co-anchor the show. The talented broadcaster worked with Natalie Morales, Willie Geist, and Al Roker. However, after three years, she unexpectedly left the show.

Why did Tamron leave the Today show? While the broadcaster has never talked about why she left the show, there have been speculations that her exit was because of the show creators bringing Megyn Kelly as the main anchor. The changes meant that the Texas-born broadcaster was to take a “lesser role” on the show.

The changes did not sit well with Tamron, and she decided to end her contract with NBC and the show. Thanks to her experience co-hosting the show, she started her talk show, with a similar approach in 2019.

As the host of the Tamron Hall Show

In 2019, the broadcaster premiered the Tamron Hall Show. The show’s signature for the past two years has been topical issues and entertaining conversations. However, the show disappeared on the show for two months in 2020.

Did the Tamron Hall Show get cancelled? No. However, the production was suspended due to the pandemic. According to Deadline, the halting of production was not new as other similar talk shows had earlier taken a similar break.

However, after a few months of being off-air, the show returned. In the new format, most of the interviews are through video calls. Other than the guests interacting with the virtual audience, Tamron continues to be the show’s main creator.

Tamron as an anti-domestic violence campaigner

While the murder of her sister Renate is still a mystery — regarding who did it — it opened the TV star’s eyes to the increasing numbers of domestic violence cases. In 2014, she launched the popular campaigned dubbed Shine a Light. Apart from raising funds for the affected victims, the TV star tours schools and colleges across the USA spreading awareness.

She has also worked with other institutions in her anti-domestic violence campaigns. For example, Tamron worked with Day One New York in their fundraising efforts. Thanks to her mobilisation skills, the institution raised over $40,000.

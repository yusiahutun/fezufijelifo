How much do NBA referees make? Salaries and everything you should know

The NBA is one of the most intriguing modern-day sport. Due to its massive fan base, the matches rack up significant income ranging from tickets sales, advertisements and a whole lot more. Fans of the sport mostly know of top players in the NBA and how much they are worth. On the other hand, the referees are usually sidelined, and not much is known of them. So how much do NBA referees make?

Similar to referees in other sports, NBA referees face a lot of criticism when officiating matches. If it is a high profile match, the pressure is more intense, but a fat paycheck comes with it. NBA referees' salaries vary with the level of skill and experience each possesses. With that, basketball referee salary is classified according to the referee's professional prowess.

How much do NBA referees make per season?

Professional referees usually take home $7,000 for officiating a single NBA game. Moreover, they take a more oversized bag when officiating playoff matches taking $9,000. In addition, when the chance comes to officiate a semifinal or a final, a professional referee gets a whopping $29,000 for that game.

On the other hand, entry-level referees are paid less each, bagging $2000 for overseeing a match. Women referees are the least paid NBA referees as they receive $450 to $500 per match. So how much does NBA referee make a year? Annually, professional and junior referees make $150,000 to $550,000 while women referees bag $16,000.

Who are the highest-paid NBA refs?

From the look of things, the more experienced NBA referees pocket a significant sum for overseeing a match. Here are some of the highest-paid NBA refs today.

1. James Capers

James Capers was born on November 8, 1961, and he is 59 years old as of 2021. He is a professional level referee born in Chicago. Over the course of his career, he has officiated over 1,357 games in 24 seasons. Moreover, he has overseen over 135 playoff games, including 10 Finals.

In addition, Capers officiated the 2018 NBA All-Star game. James has six years of experience working in the CBA, including three CBA Finals and two CBA All-Star games. The NBA referee salary is estimated at $550,000 per year.

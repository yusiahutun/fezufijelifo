How much do pro volleyball players make?

According to Sapling, a professional volleyballer’s salary averages $65,000 yearly. However, the most renowned players in the world from different countries make millions in dollars per year in salaries and endorsements.

Entry-level professional volleyball players can expect to earn between $12,000 and $55,000 per year, while seasoned players can earn up to $100,000 yearly. But, of course, it depends where you are playing from, the position you play, and how much you are good at the game.

Professional volleyballers take part in tournaments and events nationally and internationally. They may also specialize in the sport's niches, including indoor, outdoor or beach volleyball. In the United States of America, young volleyballers are scouted into improvement programs like the USA Volleyball National Team Development Program, which builds their skills.

Professional volleyball players' salaries rely upon various factors that make the highest-earners super athletes. These factors include the foreigner's league quota, skill level, and position. So, how do these factors affect the player’s earnings and endorsements?

Foreigner quota

The foreigner quota is one of the most integral factors regarding a professional player's earnings. Every volleyball league has rules specifying the number of foreigners that can play on contract for a club. Of course, the lower the number of foreigners in a club, the higher their salaries.

Foreign players in Russia and Poland make more money than other countries. The primary reason is that the foreigner quota for the two countries is low, with 2 players for Russia and 3 for Poland.

Other countries like Germany don't have a foreigner quota. For example, France only allows 6 per team, while Italy allows only 4.

The skill level

You will be in high demand if you are highly skilled, and teams would want to pay more for you to join them. Therefore, you will be able to determine how much money your contract will be worth.

How much do pro volleyball players make in Europe?

Many professional volleyballers look forward to joining the different European leagues as it is one of the most lucrative. But of course, where you end up will largely determine how much you earn.

Professional volleyballers in Europe earn an average of between $13,000 and $100,000. Some big stars earn a salary as high as $1 million.

Professional volleyball players' earnings in Italy

The Italian Superliga has the crème de la crème in the game and has the highest level of volleyball in the world. This fact, of course, translates to the incredible talent and highly valued players.

Experienced players in the Italian leagues earn around $1 million yearly, while rookies make around $100,000 annually. Therefore, clubs pay a lot of money for top talent to stay on top of the league, considering the high competition.

How much do female volleyball players make?

Like men, the salaries for professional female volleyballers vary significantly depending on the league, skill level, and player position. An entry-level female player should earn between $10,000 and $25,000. Experienced ones can make up to $100,000 a year.

Some women earn more than a million dollars a year. For instance, China's Zhu Ting is one of the best volleyballers in the league and earns a salary of approximately $2 million annually.

How to remove call barring and SMS barring from your Android phone

Android phones have become an integral part of people's daily lives, offering a versatile and customisable platform that empowers users with a wide range of features and functionalities. Developed by Google, Android is an open-source operating system that powers a vast majority of smartphones and tablets across the globe. However, many people don't know how to remove call barring from this kind of phone.

Today, many people are embracing the use of Android phones. Samsung, renowned for its Galaxy series, and Google's Pixel phones are prominent examples of Android-powered devices that seamlessly integrate this technology. As users explore their Android phones' vast capabilities, removing call barring becomes essential for unrestricted communication.

How to remove call barring on Android phone

Have you ever been in a place where you only need quiet and meditation? If yes, such moments require you to use call barring on your phone. Call barring on an Android phone is like putting up a temporary "No Entry" sign for certain types of calls. It's a feature that lets you control incoming and outgoing calls based on different criteria.

For example, you can block incoming, outgoing, or international calls. It's handy when you want some quiet time, prevent accidental calls, or manage your expenses by avoiding international calls. So, call barring is like your phone's bouncer, helping you decide which calls get through and which ones don't.

So how do you turn off barring calls from your Android phone like Samsung Galaxy? Here are the steps to follow:

  • Open the phone app on your Android phone.
  • Look for the three-dot menu or settings gear icon within the Phone app.
  • Navigate to settings or call settings.
  • Find the option for call barring or a similar term in the settings.
  • Once in the call barring settings, you should see options for different types of call barring (e.g., incoming calls and outgoing calls).
  • Disable the call-barring options you want to turn off by unchecking or toggling off the corresponding settings.
  • Enter PIN (if required): Some carriers may require you to enter a PIN to modify call-barring settings. If prompted, enter the correct PIN.
  • Save your changes and exit the settings.

    How to remove call restrictions

    Many Android phone users also use Safaricom lines to make calls. Instead of using call-barring restrictions on the Android settings, you can also find options on the phone sim card settings, such as those of the Safaricom line. Here are the steps for removing incoming call barring while using a Safaricom network:

  • Dial #330*0000# and press the call button.
  • If you have changed the barring password, dial #330* call barring password# and press the call button. The barring password is 0000.
  • If you changed it and you cannot remember it, contact Safaricom self-care or send them a message on their social media platforms on Twitter or Facebook.
  • Follow them and wait for them to follow you back, and then DM your phone number, and you will be sorted.
  • You can also call 100 from your mobile phone and follow the steps.

    Alternatively, if you are using Android phones, follow the following steps to remove message barring:

  • Open the messaging app on your Android phone.
  • Look for the three-dot menu icon or the settings gear within the messaging app.
  • Navigate to settings or messaging settings.
  • Find the option for message barring, SMS barring, or something similar. It might be under additional settings or a similar section.
  • Once in the message barring settings, disable the options related to outgoing or incoming SMS by unchecking or toggling off the corresponding settings.
  • Enter PIN (if required).
  • Save the changes and exit the settings.

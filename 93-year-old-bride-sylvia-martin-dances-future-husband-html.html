93-year-old bride Sylvia Martin dances with future husband

Bride-to-be Sylvia Martin, 93, from Canberra, sent social media into a frenzy in early June when she asked Facebook to help pick out her wedding dress.

The great-grandmother, who will soon marry her longtime friend Frank Raymond, 88, went shopping for the ideal gown before posting a snap of four different options on social media. 

Now, after settling on option C, a stunning $349 Sacha Drake short-sleeved lace gown, she is excitedly preparing for her July 25 wedding. 

In a segment on the Today Show on Tuesday, Sylvia and Frank were captured sharing a heartwarming dance together in their house before sitting down together and opening up about their relationship.

'This is my home and she asked me in my home "Will you marry me?",' Frank said. 

'So we are going to be married in our house.'

Frank, who has been asking for Sylvia's hand in marriage for 20 years, showed off Sylvia's ring - a ring he had given her several years prior. 

Out of respect for her deceased husband, Sylvia had always declined. But she changed her mind last year.

'First thing in the morning, if I've let go or he's turned over I look for his hand.'

Sylvia who will say 'I do' by a lake surrounded by family and friends, went shopping in search of the ideal gown in late May. 

With four dresses to choose from, Sylvia took to Facebook a week later to ask for help with her decision - the heartwarming post featuring a collage of her trying on each of the gowns.

Since being shared by clothing retailer Birdsnest, where Sylvia tried on the dresses, her Facebook fashion parade has gathered more than 10,000 responses.

Asked to vote A, B, C or D for the four choices, online opinions were split.

In the end, Sylvia chose option C, a classically elegant short-sleeved lace gown.

But Sylvia also took away choice D for less formal occasions after the 'Bird Keepers by Design' $99.95 cape dress was the pick of Facebook users.

'A year ago, Sylvia took ill and moved to Canberra to be near her family, and Frank thought all hope was lost,' the Facebook post read.

'When Sylvia recovered, she found Frank, who was living in Adelaide, and convinced him to move to Canberra to be with her.'

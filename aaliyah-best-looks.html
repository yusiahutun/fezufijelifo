MTV Awards, 2000

KMazur//Getty Images

“I was on an extended vacation in Italy [when I] got an email from my agent about Aaliyah wanting to go to the MTV Awards, [asking] if I would be able to bring some things by for her to see,” the stylist to the stars explains on how one of her most iconic looks came to fruition. “It was a last-minute request, and I was on holiday, so I called [Roberto] Cavalli in New York City and had an assistant both shop on his own, pick up, and do a fitting with Aaliyah. He met her at the hotel; Aaliyah immediately asked what I had specifically picked out for her, which ended up being the yellow-and-black dress.” -Derek Lee

Rat and Boa Fabienne Dress

Rat and Boa Fabienne Dress

If you’re hoping to re-create one of Aaliyah’s most iconic looks, Rat and Boa’s Fabienne Dress is a chic alternative (especially considering the vintage Roberto Cavalli piece is impossible to find).

Planet of the Apes World Movie Premiere, 2001

Evan Agostini//Getty Images

Low-rise jeans were spiking to popularity in the early 2000s, and there’s no denying that Aaliyah’s personal style had something to do with it. The singer sported a skinny white pair on the red carpet, dressing them up with a romantic chiffon blouse and luxe accessories like a chain belt draped across her hips. Reminiscent of something we’d see the likes of Bella Hadid wearing, this look was completed with a strappy sandal and loose beach waves.

Essence Awards, 2001

Evan Agostini//Getty Images

Aaliyah was somewhat known for wearing Roberto Cavalli’s gowns to red-carpet events. Though the black-and-yellow zebra-print ensemble she wore the year prior is more famously known, her feather-printed silk gown—complete with a bustier—by the luxury label is just as good. In addition to her business relationship with fashion brand Tommy Hilfiger, the singer-turned-actress and her stylist had quite a thing for Cavalli.

Roberto Cavalli Printed Silk Corseted Trained Evening Dress

MTV 20th Anniversary, 2001

Ron Galella, Ltd.//Getty Images

Staying true to her laid-back style, the singer sported a pair of low-rise jeans in a medium wash with a leather laser-cut bustier. Known for her ability to find that balance between effortless and chic, she truly embodied her approach to fashion with this look.

Romeo Must Die Premiere, 2000

Steve Granitz//Getty Images

For the premiere of the star’s breakout movie role, Romeo Must Die, she wore an embellished sheer top with a knee-length silhouette. The singer layered it over straight-leg pants with flat sandals to balance the glamorous feel. This is a great example of how to pull off wearing a dress over pants without it feeling too bulky or mismatched.

Anastasia Movie Premiere, 1997

Ron Galella, Ltd.//Getty Images

Before Hailey Bieber wore Bottega’s fringed shearling coat with baggy jeans while out and about in New York City, Aaliyah sported the combo in all white on the red carpet in the late '90s. Though one of her earlier ensembles, the look set the tone for the more effortless approach to glam that the star would take as her career continued to blossom. Now, it’s become one of her most distinct looks.

The Forum, 1997

Jeffrey Mayer//Getty Images

In quintessential Aaliyah fashion, the singer sported a pair of baggy vinyl pants—Calvin Klein boxers exposed, of course—for her 1997 performance at The Forum in Los Angeles. But it's the body wrapping, bikini-style top she wore with it that cemented her status as the ultimate trendsetter. With the midriff-flossing trend making a big comeback for summer 2021, the Romeo Must Die star served up ample inspiration for fans to pull from.

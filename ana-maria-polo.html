Ana María Polo- Biography

Ana Maria Polo is a Cuban-American lawyer and television personality best known as the arbiter of Telemundo’s famous reality show “Caso Cerrado.”

What is the Net Worth of Ana María Polo? Salary, Earnings

Polo has a net worth of roughly $1.4 million as of 2022, according to reputable sources, with the majority of her money derived from her legal career and her participation in the “Caso Cerrado” show. Her annual salary is expected to be $350,000.

Ana María Polo- Birth, Age, Ethnicity, Siblings, Education

Ana Maria Polo González was born on April 11, 1959, in Havana, Cuba, to Joaquin Polo and Delia Polo. Her family relocated to Miami, Florida, and eventually to Puerto Rico, where she completed her elementary and high school studies. She also attended Academia Del Perpetuo Socorro and performed in musicals such as “Godspell” and “Show Boat” in Puerto Rico.

She was also a member of the Jubilee chorus, which was asked by Pope Paul VI to perform during the Holy Year of 1975. Polo attended the University of Miami after graduating from Florida International University with a degree in political science. He earned a Juris Doctorate in law in 1987.

Career Following graduation, Polo became a member of the Florida Bar and began practicing in Miami-Dade County as a family mediator in a variety of issues ranging from divorce, adoptions, and custody to domestic violence. She also worked for the Coral Gables, Florida-based law firm Emmanuel Perez & Associates, P.A.

Ana María Polo- Relationship, Married Life

Polo married a man ten years her senior when she was 19 years old, according to sources. His name is unknown to the media, but the marriage terminated in divorce shortly after they lost a baby. She hasn’t remarried, but she does have a son named Peter, whom she apparently adopted and reared on her own and the circumstances of whom haven’t been made public.
Polo was diagnosed with severe breast cancer in 2003, which resulted in the removal of her ovaries, right breast, and thyroid gland. She has since given presentations and helped raise funds for cancer research.

In terms of her romantic life, Polo made headlines following a story in a Mexican tabloid magazine that said that she is dating her personal assistant and executive producer of “Caso Cerrado,” Marlene Kay. Several images of Polo and Kay surfaced in the media, and word spread that the pair had been together for over 25 years.

Ana María Polo- Professional Career

Since 2001, she has acted as an arbiter in the reality TV series “Caso Cerrado,” a famous show that has had a significant impact on her fame. Polo has also worked as a family law expert on various TV shows such as “Maria Laria Talk Show,” “Despierta America,” “El Show de Cristina,” and “America en Vivo,” as well as the CMQ Angel Martin radio show. She’s also authored pieces for the online magazine Obsidiana.com about various family issues.

In 2010, she established “Persiguiendo Injusticias,” a new Hispanic television program dedicated to uncovering “cold case” crimes. The same year, she published her first book, “Querida Dra. Polo Las Cartas Secretas de Caso Cerrado,” based on letters received to her by viewers. The book was a huge commercial success.

Polo has received various accolades, including the Mickey Leland Humanitarian Achievement Award, which recognizes businesses and individuals in the communications profession who have demonstrated a humanitarian commitment.

She was appointed the first Hispanic ambassador for Stand Up To Cancer because she is a breast cancer survivor who has collaborated with groups working to discover a cure for cancer.

She has also been involved in campaigning for human rights and against discrimination, being named one of the world’s most powerful Hispanic personalities, which led to her being invited to participate in the Human Rights Organization’s Americans for Marriage Equality video campaign in 2014.

“Caso Cerrado”

The popular show debuted on the Spanish-language network Telemundo on April 2, 2001, under the title “Sala de Parejas” (Couple’s Court). It dealt with marital dispute arbitration between litigants, with Polo acting as the host, i.e. the arbitrator. When the show extended to deal with instances outside of the home sphere in 2005, it was renamed “Caso Cerrado” (Case Closed). Since then, the half-hour show has dealt with resolving various family disagreements, in which Polo usually handles up to two cases and serves as an arbiter for people addressing various marital concerns, attempting to resolve their arguments, and providing them with a solution at the end of the show.
Prior to appearing on the show, the litigants must sign contracts stating that they will follow Polo’s verdict. Polo not only hosted the show, but he also wrote and performed the theme song.

“Caso Cerrado” has become one of Telemundo’s highest-rated series, and Polo has won over the hearts of fans by maintaining a strong connection with the Hispanic community from all over the world. ‘I adore doing “Caso Cerrado,” especially because it has given me a really unique connection with the Hispanic audience that I would like to preserve for as long as I can,’ she remarked in an interview. It is the first Spanish-language program to receive a Daytime Entertainment Emmy nomination.

During 2017, the show was broadcast on Fox TV stations in Austin, Dallas, Los Angeles, and Phoenix, as well as on Facebook Live across the country.

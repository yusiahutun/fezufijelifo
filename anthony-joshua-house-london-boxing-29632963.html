Anthony Joshua 'spending 25million' on huge house in centre of London

After defeating Jermaine Franklin on Saturday night, Anthony Joshua looks set to reward himself with a huge £25 million property in central London.

The 33-year-old hit the very top of the sport as world champion but despite his success in the ring, remained in a council flat on Golders Green for some time.

But The Sun reports AJ is in the process of splashing cash on a new property on the famous Bond Street, in the heart of the capital. House prices around Bond Street had an average of around £6m in 2022 and Joshua looks ready to splash the cash on a stunning new pad.

The former WBA, IBF and WBO champ is rumoured to be worth in excess of £120million and pocketed up to another £10million from his latest bout.

That is thanks to the giant fight purses and multiple endorsement deals he has agreed, including his latest DAZN deal which broadcasted his bout with Franklin. The five-year partnership with the global sports streaming service is reportedly worth £100 million a year.

The move marked the end of Joshua’s contract with Sky Sports, which had been in place throughout his professional career. “I am entering a new phase in my career with a new training environment, new coaches and now a new broadcaster,” Joshua said once the deal was made.

“Negotiations at this level take time, so I am pleased to have it all wrapped up and now I can fully focus on giving the fans and DAZN what they want – knockouts in the glamour division. I’ve been working with DAZN and following its progress for a long time. We have enjoyed a really successful partnership in the US for many years and I know the team and understand the passion and drive of the business.”

The growing sports streaming platform showed AJ’s vital win as he prevailed via unanimous decision on his return to the O2 Arena. The judges scored the bout 118-111, 117-111, 117-111 in favour of the former two-time world champion as he got his career back on track, claiming his first win since 2020.

Post match Joshua said he has his sights on WBC champion Tyson Fury - answering fans' chants for the clash after his unanimous decision victory. He said: "You know me, I like to provide for the fans. I know who the fans want. Who do the fans want?

"They said Fury? The ball is in his court, I will 100 per cent be honoured to compete for the WBC heavyweight championship of the world. I stand here and say that proudly, it would be an honour, so wherever you are if you're listening. You know my management, you know my promoter.

"We've had dialect before, so let's continue this and hopefully we can get this on sooner rather than later. We ain't getting any younger."

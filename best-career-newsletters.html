The 8 Best Career Newsletters

There are millions of career-related articles online, and we can all agree that there's far too much information out there. They serve as a free resource that you can use to learn career development and job search skills.

These newsletters can help you hone your craft as an employee through tips, advice, and suggestions on how you can improve your job search. We've tested several career newsletters to find the best ones for you.

1. The Forbes Careers

Forbes offers tailored newsletters for its readers, ranging from career advice to cutting-edge innovation making the news. The Forbes Career newsletter is a weekly email that delivers the latest career news, advice, and inspiration from Forbes experts to your inbox.

But it isn't just about helping you get a job—it's also about helping you understand all about salary trends, work-life balance, and how to make sure that your current job is right for you.

With every issue, they share curated picks for the week, including articles and videos—so you can learn more about what drives your favorite companies, get inspiration for your next career move, and hear from industry leaders about their experiences.

2. The Balance Careers

The Balance Careers is a great website to keep up with the latest trends in career advice and job search. It provides tips and resources to help you find jobs, advance your career, and make better decisions about your future.

The Balance Today daily newsletter is not necessarily focused on careers per se, but you can expect to find the latest finance-related news and advice, including a curated collection of "Editor's Picks" and "Term of the Day".

Their website offers a wealth of resources—you can get access to free e-books and guides on job search topics—getting hired, creating an effective cover letter, or how to stand out in an interview. Whether you're looking for tips on how to interview well or how much to save for retirement, this newsletter has got you covered.

3. The Muse

The Muse Career newsletter is chock-full of career advice, tools, and resources from the Muse team. It's a great way to keep up with all things career-related—from industry trends and company announcements to personal development tips and advice on how to ace your next interview.

They also include exclusive thought leadership content from industry experts on topics such as managing your career during periods of change and developing skills that will make you more marketable in today's economy, or why you should stop worrying about your salary right now. It also keeps you updated with the latest job listings.

4. Morning Brew - Sidekick

Sidekick is a fun, informative, and helpful website for anyone looking to get the most out of their workday. They have a couple of newsletters and podcasts—the Morning Brew Daily and Money Scoop offer the top stories on topics like technology, innovation, and personal finance.

Sidekick provides you with tips and tricks to be more productive at work and feel more prepared for any situation. Sidekick is about finding a balance between your personal and professional lives, and they include some fun stuff too!

From foodie finds to drink recipes, you can delve into things that make you happy outside of work. The Sidekick newsletter has four sections: Optimize, OffHours, Eat+Drink, and Work Life, so you can stay on top of your career and still feel like you're in control of your life.

5. Remote.Co

Remote.Co is a remote work community and newsletter that helps you find your next job, hire people for your team, and learn about the best ways to work remotely.

It provides tips and resources on how to manage your remote workers, along with tools to help you find the right talent and keep them happy. If you're looking to develop your professional acumen, the website also provides links to recommended online courses for various industries.

Their newsletter features remote jobs from all over the world. It includes a mix of positions—from entry-level to senior—and industries—from design to technology and more.

6. WorkITDaily

The WorkITDaily Dose newsletter is full of interesting, actionable advice that can help you navigate your career confidently. You'll learn how to build networking skills, improve your resume and cover letter, find a job that fits your personality type, and get up to speed with interview strategies.

With a free membership, there are plenty of other benefits as well—access to paid events as well as free resources and templates that will help you succeed at work. WorkITDaily covers all aspects of professional development, from finding an internship to getting a promotion or learning how to negotiate salary increases.

7. Career Contessa

Career Contessa is a newsletter that is aimed at helping women be more successful at work, by providing them with useful resources—guides, books, and career coaching that help them be more fulfilled and healthy in their careers. They publish thought-provoking articles on topics like work-life balance, confidence, and career advancement.

The newsletter is a trusted resource that helps women professionals be their best selves—on the job and in life. With articles about everything from how to be successful at work to negotiating for a salary raise, it's like having your own personal career coach in your inbox.

8. TED

While TED is not solely a career-focused website, it has resources for business leaders, job-seekers, entrepreneurs, and innovators who want to stay in the know about current events. They publish short videos, known as TED Talks.

Their newsletter keeps you updated about talks, and workshops happening around TEDx events across the world. TED Newsletters (TED Recommends and Daily Email) include insightful content on topics ranging from technology to design to science and innovation. Stay on Track With Your Career

These career newsletters make interesting reads because they are written by experts in their field—they give you helpful tips that you can use to further your career goals.

The newsletters touch on a variety of topics—from improving your job search to making the most of your workday. If you want to get a leg up as you try and move your career forward, these career newsletters are sure to prove indispensable.

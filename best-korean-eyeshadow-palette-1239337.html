    Before we hop onto the best way of creating a Korean eye makeup look, check out some quick tips on how to choose the best eyeshadow palette for yourself.

    How to Choose the Best Korean Eyeshadow Palette?

    Formula: Not all eyeshadows deliver you the same results. From its texture and finish to pigmentation, there are many factors to consider. Once you have downsized your theme for colors and shades, check for the type of finishes offered in a palette. The more the better! If you have a combination of mattes, glitters, metallics, and shimmers, you can create every look to wear from AM to PM. Pick a soft yet pigmented texture that is easy to blend and build, and additionally has a long stay.

    Talking about the formula and finish, our contributor Maya Adivi, a certified makeup artist, says, “I think Korean powder eyeshadows are far more common and often have better wear times so they're worth the effort to master.” 

    She adds, “As far as ingredients are concerned, Korean eyeshadows are often quite gentle on sensitive eyes. You might want to avoid fragranced formulas or eyeshadows that contain crimson red or carmine pigment, as those can be allergenic.”

    Pigment: An essential thing to note for Korean palettes is that the shadows are only mid-pigmented. Since the preference is a more natural, muted, and sheer finish; the colors do not have an intense payoff or pigment. However, when it comes to shimmers, the formula is more adhesive.  

    Palette features: If you have sensitive skin, make sure the shadows are hypoallergenic. You may also look for options that are water and sweat-proof, non-clumpy, and crease-proof. For an added benefit, go for the palettes that offer a dual-end brush so you can easily carry it and apply the makeup with ease.

    How to Apply Korean Eyeshadow Palette?

    Step 1: Using warm browns, soft pinks, and plush peaches you can create a minimal and natural base for your eyeshadow. These sheer yet matte powders will help you achieve a flattering eyeshadow on the eyes. You can use a soft brush or your fingers to gently dab the color around the eyes in a circular motion. 

    Step 2: The idea is to make the eyes look younger and brighter. The next step is to add a little pop. You need a brighter and more vivid shade than your base or use glitter shadow to create a heart-like spot. Begin by applying the eyeshadow on top of your eyelids and smudge it below the water line. 

    Step 3: For enhanced depth and definition, use dark or somber eyeshadow and smudge it around the outer corners of the eyes, as well as on the inner corners. Try a similar shading effect on your lower lash line to finish the look. 

    Pro hack: Staying under the subdued memo, you can use a darker shade from your palette and apply it as an eyeliner for that puppy-eye look or simply use a regular liner for more glam. 

    Step 4: Finish off your eye makeup by coating your lashes with mascaras and drawing the brows.

    FAQ's

    What makes a Korean eyeshadow palette different from others?

    Korean palettes are usually compact with sheer color payoff and mid-pigment. The hues and glitters are not too flashy and ideal for naturally enhanced makeup.

    Are Korean eyeshadow palettes suitable for all skin tones?

    Yes, the palettes are suitable for all skin tones. The colors have a sheer effect and are buildable to help you achieve the desired shade. However, there are more Korean eyeshadow palettes suited for cooler skin tones than warmer ones.

    Can Korean eyeshadow palettes be used for both day and night looks?

    With light hues, smoky browns, and glitters available in one palette, you can use the Korean eyeshadow pans to create looks that last from AM to PM.

    Do Korean eyeshadow palettes come with applicators or brushes?

    Most of the Korean eyeshadow palettes come with dual-ended brushes or sponge applicators that allow easy application and blending of shadows.

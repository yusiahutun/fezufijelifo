1950s Teen Pop Icon Bobby Rydell Dead At 79

Bobby Rydell, the singer who became a teen idol in the late 1950s with hits such as “Wild One” and his cover of “Volare,” died on Tuesday in Abington, Pennsylvania. He was 79. An obituary published on his website said that he died from non-Covid-19 related pneumonia complications. Born Robert Ridarelli in Philadelphia in 1942, Rydell got his first big break when he won Paul Whiteman’s TV Teen Club televised talent show in 1950 and earned a spot on the show’s cast.

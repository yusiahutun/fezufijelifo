Chanel bag experts reveal tiny design element that will help you spot a FAKE

If you're looking to invest in a pre-owned Chanel bag, it's imperative that you be wary of its serial number before you buy, luxury experts have revealed. 

The warning was shared in an Instagram Reel posted by The Gestreon, a London-based brand that offers expertly curated, pre-loved luxury fashion items to its customers.

In the clip, one of their experts claimed that those who are buying pre-loved Chanel bags should avoid those that have the serial number, '10218184,' due to the fact that it is one of the most replicated numbers for counterfeit bags. 

'Don't buy a Chanel bag with this serial number,' the handbag authority explained, holding up the brand's classic Double Flap bag in black with gold hardware.

'This is the most counterfeited Chanel serial number.'

The brand first began dating their handbags in 1984, according to Lux Second Chance, and the serial code indicates when exactly the bag was made.

However, with many fake Chanel replicas on the market these days, it's hard to tell exactly what you're buying when purchasing from resellers. 

Vidyuth Srinivasan, cofounder and CEO of Entrupy, a company that enlists advanced artificial intelligence to authenticate and verify designer bags and luxury goods, told DailyMail.com that they have seen more than 1,800 'unique bags' with this exact serial number, none of which were authentic by their standards. 

He gathered that this serial code was 'specific to one style of Chanel bags.'

According to wholesaler pre-owned luxury brand LePrix, the specific code brought to light by The Gestreon can be found in a number of counterfeit Chanel bags, but is not the only feature that potential buyers should be concerned with. 

'Counterfeiters often use 10218184 and it is seen frequently in the Chanel Classic Flap styles but can be found in other styles as well,' Emily Erkel, cofounder and CMO of LePrix, told DailyMail.com. 

'However, this does not mean a bag that has a different serial number is authentic or not authentic. It depends on a number of different authentication check points,' Erkel continued.

Some of these 'check points' include looking at hardware on the bag like zippers, hardware and quilting. 

Erkel furthered that the serial number should only be 'one part' of the process when trying to authenticate a pre-owned Chanel bag.

Alone, bags that are pre-loved can still cost thousands of dollars and can be a major investment for some. 

'Verification cannot solely rely on serial numbers and authentication cards alone,' the co-founder told DailyMail.com. 

'It is important to note that especially vintage Chanel, serial numbers can fall off over time as well so authenticators look for multiple points to determine authenticity.'

Srinivasan agreed with Erkel's sentiments about the verification process. 

He said that there were multiple steps one should take in order to ensure that the bag was real, like asking questions of the bag's seller. 

'Serial numbers alone are not good indicators of whether a bag is authentic or otherwise,' Srinivasan told DailyMail.com. 'For newer bags, there are no serial labels, but that doesn't mean that the item is not authentic.'

Speaking to DailyMail.com, Erkel explained that it's important to take a look at the pre-loved bag's leather and quilting. 

'For lambskin, the leather feels soft to touch whereas Caviar has a pebbled, textured appearance,' she explained.

'Other points to look carefully also include the quilting, zipper pulls, hardware, CC turnlock and interior of the bag.'

'Getting it certified or asking for a certificate of authenticity is good practice, especially before purchasing,' the expert continued.

'A consumer should be asking sellers how are they authenticating and having a third party like Entrupy ensures that there is a non-biased assessment of the product.'

But when inspecting a serial number, it's important to keep in mind that over the years, their look and style has changed as well. 

Within the year, serial numbers can change and have 'different formats,' Erkel explained to DailyMail.com. 

For example, some years the zeroes have strikethroughs, while some do not. 

'Even some years, the serial numbers can differ fonts, 1s in Serif or San Serif font,' Erkel said.

If you do come across this serial number on your vintage or pre-loved bag journey, you should be taking extra steps to make sure that you're not purchasing a counterfeit, according to the experts. 

'This serial is so prevalent among the counterfeits that if you come across it, you should ask for an authentication certificate like Entrupy's so you as a consumer are protected,' Srinivasan explained.

You can also take a look at the bag's authenticity card as well, giving it a once-over to check the 'fonts and spacing symmetry,' Erkel added.

She explained that the serial number on the card should always match the serial number on the bag. 

When it comes to vintage Chanel bags, though, the protocol is a bit different. 

'For vintage bags, oftentimes the authenticity card can go missing over time so authentication experts do not rely solely on these to authenticate and use additional methods,' she advised.

Both Erkel and Srinivasan suggested working with a third-party verification service, particularly one that's trusted and verified.

'We strongly recommend buying from a verified, trusted source that has a very strict and transparent authentication policy,' Erkel told DailyMail.com, whose brand, LePrix, ensures their bags are inspected and authenticated twice. 

'If you do venture to buy a pre-owned bag on a peer-to-peer platform, some of the larger platforms also authenticate if the item is over a certain price.

'When in doubt, work with an independent, trusted third-party like Entrupy, Real Authentication or Bababebi to ensure your bag is authenticated.'

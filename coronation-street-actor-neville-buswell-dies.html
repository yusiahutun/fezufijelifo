Coronation Street actor Neville Buswell dies at the age of 77

The actor was best known for playing Ray Langton

    Sad news from the world of soaps; Coronation Street actor Neville Buswell has died at the age of 77. The TV star, who was best known for playing Ray Langton in the ITV soap, passed away on Christmas Day. An obituary, along with a photograph, was shared by undertakers in his adopted hometown of Las Vegas this week, confirming his death. Neville first appeared on in 1966, and later returned in 1968, working as a regular cast member for ten years. His character, womaniser Ray, famously married Deirdre Barlow, only to abandon her with a young daughter, Tracey, and move to Amsterdam.

    Neville Buswell and Anne Kirkbride as Ray and Deirdre

    After being written out of the show in 1978, Neville moved to Las Vegas with his American wife and worked as a croupier in a casino, the reports. He did, however, return to in 2005 for a six-week run, with his character dying of lung cancer at Deirdre and Ken Barlow's wedding reception in the Rover's Return, leaving daughter Tracey heartbroken.

    Coronation Street's Katie McGlynn talks about the emotional end of her character

    News of Neville's death comes just one day before the National Television Awards. The show has been nominated in three categories; Serial Drama, Serial Drama Performance for Katie McGlynn and her heartbreaking portrayal of Sinead Tinker, and Newcomer, with Peter Ash nominated for his turn as Paul Foreman. The 2020 National Television Awards will air live on ITV on 28th January, from The O2, London. judge David Walliams is hosting, having taken over from Dermot O'Leary, who was at the helm for the last ten years. "I am thrilled to be the new host of the NTAs - and will ensure I also win them all," David said of the news in a statement.

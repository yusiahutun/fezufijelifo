Ethan Hawke's daughter Maya 'couldn't care less' over 'sexual' scenes for dad's movie

Ethan Hawke insisted that it’s not “weird” directing his daughter getting felt up in graphic scenes in his latest film.

The 52-year-old Dead Poets Society star directed biographical flick, Wildcat, which premiered at the Telluride Film festival earlier this month and is streaming on Amazon Prime.

Ethan’s daughter Maya took the lead as American novelist and recluse, Flannery O'Connor, as she struggled to publish her first book.

She also played six other characters from the author’s short stories, which included some very steamy scenes.

In one of the stories named 'Parker’s Back', Maya’s character gets 'felt up' in a pick-up truck, Variety noted on Friday (September 8).

“We were so comfortable with it. I couldn’t care less,” he revealed in another interview with Variety.

Maya relished the chance for her female character to express her sexuality, even if it was in front of her A-lister father.

“Young actresses very rarely get that opportunity to be brilliant, complicated, mean, sexual, asexual”, she said.

And the pair put in guardrails to ensure their was no awkwardness.

“We made sure to have an intimacy coordinator on set for them,” the 25-year-old actress said.

“So that they felt safe and comfortable and not like they were being spied on …”

Ethan interjected: “... by some creepy dad.”

With Pulp Fiction actress Uma Thurman as a mum, and Ethan as a dad, a flurry of social media users have branded Maya as a ‘nepo baby’.

But she became an instant hit when she played Steve Harrington’s best bud, Robin Buckley, from season three of Netflix’s Stranger Things.

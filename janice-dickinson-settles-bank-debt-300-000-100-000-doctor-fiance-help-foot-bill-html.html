Janice Dickinson has agreed a $100,000 payment plan with a bank to which she owed $300,000 in bounced checks.

The reduced payment plan comes after the 59-year-old was declared bankrupt in April 2013 with $1million in debt.

But despite agreeing to pay back just a third of what she owed, Janice had to enlist the help of her fiancé to meet her obligations.

Settlement reached: Janice Dicksinson, shown in February in New York City, has reached a pay agreement with City National Bank over more than $300,000 in overdraft checks

According to bankruptcy documents seen by TMZ, Dr. Robert Gerner agreed to pay the initial amounts.

Gerner, a leading psychopharmacology expert, helped Janice out after she reached the payment plan with City National Bank.

Documents reveal how the Los Angeles-based bank coldly calculated that it would be tough to collect from Dickinson.

Happy couple: Janice, shown with fiance Robert Gerner in February 2013 in West Hollywood, California, agreed to pay back $100,000 of the more than $300,000 owed

Bank officials said it would be hard 'due to debtor's age, lack of assets and lack of income'.

The outspoken reality star declared bankruptcy after accumulating unpaid tax bills and doctors' bills for plastic surgery procedures.

She owed more than $8,000 to Beverly Hills dermatologist Arnold Klein and was more than $8,000 in debt with the Beverly Hills Anti-Aging Center at the time of her bankruptcy filing.

Big ring: Janice, shown earlier this month in West Hollywood, flashed her new engagement ring

Dickinson also had unpaid taxes of more than $500,000 due to California, New York and the Internal Revenue Service that dated back more than a decade, according to a report by the New York Post.

The former top model has sued pharmacy giant Rite Aid for injuries she claimed she suffered last July after being struck in the head by a parking barrier.

Dickinson went to China for stem cell treatment last year to treat injuries related to the incident.

Kate Middleton rewears Self-Portrait blazer dress for Jubilee concert

The Duchess of Cambridge joined the rest of the royal family for the Platinum Party at the Palace, a live concert staged outside Buckingham Palace, on night three of the official Platinum Jubilee celebrations.

For the event, which she attended alongside husband Prince William and her two eldest children, Prince George and Princess Charlotte, the royal looked to her existing wardrobe and rewore an elegant Self-Portrait midi dress. The tailored piece features a bouclé jacket with an adjustable belt, and an attached pleated chiffon skirt with a lace trim.

Kate previously wore the classic look - which retails for £400 - at a Kensington Palace reception back in September for Hold Still, her moving photography book which captured life during the pandemic.

The duchess is a fan of the contemporary London-based brand, which is helmed by the Malaysia-born designer Han Chong, and has worn Self-Portrait designs on several occasions, including for premieres and official events. Meghan, the Duchess of Sussex, is also a fan, having also worn Self-Portrait dresses several times.

Kate and the rest of the royal family seemed to enjoy the live concert, which featured performances from the likes of Alicia Keys and Diana Ross, as well as a surprise appearance from the Queen, who enjoyed tea with Paddington Bear in a sweet comedy sketch.

BBC Studios

Kate has made several stylish appearances during the Jubilee celebrations. She took part in the annual Trooping the Colour parade wearing white Alexander McQueen; wore lemon yellow Emilia Wickstead for the Service of Thanksgiving; and then a vibrant orange Eponine London coat for a visit to Cardiff.

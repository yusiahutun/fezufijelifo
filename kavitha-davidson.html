Kavitha Davidson Bio, Age, Height, Husband, Net Worth

Kavitha Davidson is an American journalist and author. Although Kavitha is currently employed at The Athletic, she holds expertise in journalism for a variety of fields. Her work has been proven exceptional throughout her career.

Early Life

Kavitha Davidson was born to Indian parents in the late 80s and no details are disclosed about her parents. She grew up in a neighborhood in northern Manhattan called Washington Heights, USA. Her parents, who were heavily fond of sports,  shifted to the USA from India in 1981 which led to Kavitha’s subsequent interest in sports. As disclosed earlier, Kavitha completed her school education in Manhattan and later pursued Journalism at Columbia University. Moreover, she acquired a diploma in the area of City Research, Historical Past, and Political Science.

Kavitha Davidson Career

Kavitha always had a command of English. She decided that she wanted to pursue journalism and thus, went to Columbia University to work for the campus newspaper. While pursuing her course, a subject called ‘Socio-Historical Foundation of American Sports’ grabbed her attention and so, she started working as an intern for sports analytics, advertising, etc for Nielsen. She even worked on media communications for the Staten Island Yankees. After her education, she started working as a sports editor for Columbia Day by Day Spectator in 2008. After that, a columnist at ESPN and Bloomberg focused on the intersection of sports, business, culture, race, and gender.

Moreover, she was also a co-host of The Lead( The Athletic’s podcast). Her work over the years has been published in leading agencies like Rolling Stone, The Guardian, and NBC News THINK and has been noted in Best American Sports Writing. Equally, she has also put efforts into media houses like The Huffington Publish, IAC, etc. Kavitha has also been an author of an e-book titled ‘Loving Sports When They Don’t Love You Back: Dilemmas of The Modern Fan’. Currently, she sits on one of the Board of Directors of the Yogi Berra Museum.

Controversy

Being a journalist is not an easy task, because regular bigotry and death threats are common in this profession. When she reported on late developments in the ‘Penn State University child sexual abuse scandal’, she received rape threats. Overwhelmed by the reactions, she bravely came out to the media as a victim of child abuse. Not much is cited except that she was just 17 when raped by a 45-year-old man. Taking a stance, she wanted other victims to come out and be held responsible for their actions.

Kim Cattrall's shock at learning her grandfather was a BIGAMIST

Kim Cattrall is best known for her portrayal of Samantha Jones in Sex and the City whose life is an open book.

But for the British-born actress there was much about her family history that she did not know which led her to appear on this season of genealogy show Who Do You Think You Are?

Cattrall's journey led her back to Liverpool, where she was born, to find out the truth about what happened to her grandfather - who had abandoned her mother and two aunts over 70 years ago.

Family secret: Sex and the City actress Kim Cattrall was shocked to find out her grandfather was a bigamist when she appeared on TV show Who Do You Think You Are?

Abandoned: Kim's mother Shane and aunts Marjorie and Dorothy last saw their father in 1938 when Shane was just eight years old

Bigamist: Kim's grandfather George Baugh went on to marry a younger woman less than a year after leaving his family - without getting a divorce

The 54-year-old actress' mother Shane was just eight-years-old when she last saw her father in 1938. Her sisters Marjorie and Dorothy were five and two years old.

He left the family in squalid conditions where they never had enough to eat and wore tatty clothing.

But Cattrall found out a bombshell when she dared to delve into her family tree in show that aired on Friday.

Elusive: The only photo Cattrall's family had of George for years was of him peering though the window during a family wedding

Hard life: Kim pictured with her grandmother Marion who was left to raise three children in squalid conditions

Her grandfather George Baugh had fled to Manchester and married another woman less than a year after abandoning his first family - without divorcing Cattrall's grandmother - making him a bigamist.

'Wow, he's a bigamist… and he was married by a vicar in a church, the actress said upon learning about his deceit.

'I knew he was gutsy but now he's a criminal. Unbelievable.

'I'm quite angry about that. As a woman I find that quite despicable.

George had gone on to have four other children with his new wife, who he seemingly had a happy and normal life with.

Moved on: Kim's family broke down in tears at seeing the picture of George and his new family seemingly happy and without a care for the three children he left

Informative: The 54-year-old actress tracked down George's sister-in-law who was able to disclose more information about the grandfather she never knew

His new wife, Isabella Oliver – who at 21 was nine years younger than Baugh – had no idea that her husband was previously married.

Baugh could have faced seven years in prison for the crime of bigamy for not divorcing Cattral's grandmother Marian.

He later emigrated to Australia with his wife and four children in 1961 where he ran a post office and shop.

He died in 1974 and his wife passed away in 1990.

Cattrall's mother and aunts only had one picture of their father - which was a barely visible image of him peering through the drapes of a house while his whole family took a picture outside the home.

George went to astonishing lengths to cover his tracks and cut his face out of any photographs he came across.

Tip: A newspaper article from the 1980s showed a wedding dress from the Baugh family being passed down another generation which was a vital clue in the search

New life: Kim was born in Liverpool but emigrated to Canada with her mother Shaun and her father when she was just three months old

When Kim managed to track down George's second wife's sister in law - she produced a picture of him on his wedding day - but he had also cut his picture out of the image so it just showed his bride and their guests.

Kim was able to track down the Baugh side of the family by an old newspaper cutting from the 1980s which mentioned a Baugh wedding dress being passed down from generation to generation.

She contacted her aunts, George's sisters, who said that he disappeared from their life also and they never heard from him again - even though he was living just miles away from Liverpool where they grew up, in Durham, England.

Cattrall's mother and aunts broke down in tears when Kim went to report back on everything she had found out about their father.

She showed them one of the only pictures of George with his new family, seemingly on a happy family day at the beach, without a care in the world of the three young girls that he left behind - which was very emotional for the women to see.

Kim did not journey to Australia to meet her new extended family of aunts and cousins she never knew she had.

But her mother and aunts did reach out to their siblings and are now in contact with the brothers and sisters they have with their shared bigamist father.

Cattrall, whose parents moved to Canada when she was just three months old, fought back tears as she saw a photo of her grandfather for the first time.

She branded him a 'son of a b*tch' and an 'absolute rogue'.

'That's really shocking and sad, really despicable,' she said. 'No sense of responsibility of this whatsoever.'

The actress said she had found the research more difficult and emotional than expected.

Visibly upset, she said: 'This journey of finding him was more about finding the family that I do have and that's a great gift.'

10 Incredible Edible Flowers for Dressing Up Cakes and Desserts

1

Pansies and Violas

Raj Kamal

These cheerful, funny-faced flowers come in every shade from sunny yellow to pinks and purples, pastels and saturated colors. Some types have frilly petals and they offer a slight minty taste.

SHOP VIOLAS

2

Dianthus

mikroman6

Also called pinks, these pretty fringed petals look amazing on baked goods. They come in white and every shade of pink imaginable, and offer a slight clove flavor.

SHOP DIANTHUS

3

Borage

Getty Images

Delicate, lacy blue flowers from this lesser-known annual herb add cottage charm to any cake, along with a slight cucumber flavor.

SHOP BORAGE FLOWERS

4

Rose

Jacky Parker Photography

The petals of roses make gorgeous garnishes for any cake from Valentine’s Day to anniversary celebrations. Scatter them on or around cakes for a finishing touch. The mildly sweet flavor can be intense depending on the variety of rose

SHOP DRIED ROSE PETALS

5

Lavender

Getty

The tiny purple flowers of this perennial make pretty “confetti” on cakes, or you can bake them into the batter of scones. You also can use the whole stem with flowers intact to garnish the cake, and add a slight floral flavor.

SHOP DRIED LAVENDER FLOWERS

6

Marigold

Rania Reafaat / 500px

The petals of this annual can be sprinkled over baked goods for a golden sunshine-y touch and a bitter-sweet citrusy flavor. They come in various shades of orange and yellow. If you grow only one edible flower, choose marigolds because they’re such hardy plants.

SHOP MARIGOLD FLOWERS

Advertisement - Continue Reading Below

7

Nasturtium

Jacky Parker Photography

Both the leaves and flowers of this low-growing annual offer a mild, peppery bite. The flowers come in a range of colors from cream to blush to hot pink and bright orange. They don’t travel well though, so you’re better off trying to grow these easy-to-grow flowers yourself.

SHOP NASTURTIUM SEEDS

8

Bachelor Button

craig fordham

The brilliant blue, pale pink or hot pink fringed blossoms look amazing on cupcakes or cakes, with a mild cucumber flavor.

SHOP BACHELOR BUTTON FLOWERS

9

Begonia

Cris Cantón

The tiny petals of begonias look adorable topping cakes and cupcakes and offering a citrusy tang. These annuals are prolific bloomers, so they’re great in the garden from spring to frost.

SHOP BEGONIA FLOWERS

Advertisement - Continue Reading Below

10

Fuchsia

Jacky Parker Photography

These exotic-looking blooms have a tangy taste and are stunning on cakes! They come in shades of pink, purple and deep rose.

SHOP FUCHSIA FLOWERS

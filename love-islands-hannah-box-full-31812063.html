Love Island's Hannah has box full of naked pics from steamy stint at Playboy Mansion

Love Island's Hannah Elizabeth has kept all of her snaps from her Playboy Bunny era in a secret little box.

The series one star first starred on the ITV show back in 2015 but was formerly a Playboy Bunny and glamour model before that. Hannah has now entered the villa once more for the All Stars series. In an interview with Daily Star, Hannah once revealed that she still has all of her Playboy memories inside a little box.

Hannah exclusively said: “In my mum’s house, I used to have a box about this big (makes a wide gesture with her hands) and I used to take a digital camera over there. At the time, we didn’t even have iPhones - imagine I had an iPhone there! I would be getting so many videos.”

Hannah has since posted some on Instagram, but also explained how some are too X-rated to post online. She added: "I've got so much more to come from there, I'm going to keep doing posts about it because people are curious and like to see it. So, in going to share some more definitely."

"But some are too much for social media, 100% yeah, because at the parties it was 'lingerie or less' so there's loads. Instagram would be shutting my page down."

Hannah used to be employed by Hugh Hefner as a 'painted lady' at his infamous mansion. The TV star explained what a normal day there would be like for her and said: "We would go out either to the game house or the gym in the mansion and we would go really early like maybe eight o'clock we'd have to be there. We would get body painted all day whil a massive party like the most like dramatic party you've ever seen would be getting set up in the back garden.

"We were fully nude but it looked like we had clothes on. And then basically when Hef would make his appearance and he would walk down the red carpet for E! channel or whatever. We would just line up with him to do the press. Then we'd go to the party like it. It was actually nuts. I actually felt like I had clothes on though, it was so weird."

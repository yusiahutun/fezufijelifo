Charlotte McKinney bemoans the daily struggles of having big breasts

Bombshell Charlotte McKinney may be best known for her curvaceous figure, but the Carl’s Jr. model has admitted that having large breasts can be a daily struggle - especially when it comes to people asking inappropriate questions about her chest. 

The 21-year-old star reveals her top gripes about having big boobs in a funny new video created by Cosmopolitan.com - and Charlotte doesn't shy away from listing the irritating things that come along with having an ample chest. 

'It is bulls*** to ask about my bra size,' she says of one of her major complaints. 'Why do you want to know? What's it to you? I mean are you going to buy me a bra or…? I don't get it.'

However, the never-ending fascination about her breasts is just one of the things she complains about in the clip. 

One may assume that a famous model like Charlotte never has issues trying on clothes, but the former Dancing With the Stars contestant says the opposite is true when it comes to bra shopping. 

She notes that bras are often the wrong fit because different brands tend to vary in size. And if a bra is too tight, there are other embarrassing factors she has to worry about.

'Underboob, overboob, it's just never ending,' she says.

Charlotte says she has similar issues with strapless bras, which she can never keep in place. 

'All day it's like,' Charlotte says as she models how she continuously has to pull up a strapless bra.   

'It just keeps falling down,' she notes. 

The model, who has been dubbed 'the next Kate Upton', goes on to mock people who feign worry over the future of her breasts.

Bikini babe: The blonde beauty says the worry about showing too much 'underboob' and 'overboob' is 'never ending' 

Plunging neckline: Charlotte notes that strapless bras are difficult to wear because they always falls down on her and she has to constantly pick them up

'Your concerns about my boobs sagging in the future is absolute bulls***,' she explains. 'It's called a breast lift.'

Charlotte adds: 'My mom's got great boobs and she is in her 60s, so you can fix it or you can just let them sag.'

Another equally irritating questions is when people ask her why she doesn't get a breast reduction.  

'Um because I don't want one?' she says. 'I am pretty happy.'

Big break: Charlotte shot to fame and was dubbed 'the next Kate Upton' after she starred in a Carl's Jr. ad in January 

And many women can related to Charlotte's final complaint about button down shirts.

The blond beauty notes that 'buttons keep breaking' and gaps in ill-fitting shirts reveal your bra. 

'It is a never ending battle,' she notes. 

The Florida-born model rose to fame on social media, with her Instagram photo earning her profile in Esquire in 2014.

Model moves: Charlotte appeared in the most recent season of Dancing with the Stars where she and partner Keo Motsepe finished in 11th place

New career: The Florida-born star has a small role in upcoming comedy Joe Dirt 2, alongside David Spade

A year later she landed her first Carl's Jr. ad before going on to appear in a sophisticated shoot for Vanity Fair. She went on to appear in the most recent season of Dancing with the Stars where she and partner Keo Motsepe finished in 11th place.

With the Carl's Jr. commercial, an appearance on Dancing with the Stars, and several high-profile modeling jobs now under her belt, Charlotte is turning her attention towards acting - and has already landed herself a talent agent who is helping to get her out there auditioning for roles.

'I've been shooting a lot and getting into acting. I have a talent agent in California, so I've been doing some coaching and a lot of auditions,' Charlotte, who has a small role in upcoming comedy Joe Dirt 2, alongside David Spade, explained to Daily Mail Online.

'[Originally] I really just saw my career as modeling. But as I've been modeling, it kind of got boring to me, to the point where I did one little skit on [Comedy Central series] Tosh.0 and since then, I've really loved comedy and loved being on set and it's just more fun to me.'

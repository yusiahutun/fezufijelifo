All About Patti LaBelle's Marriage to Ex-Husband Armstead Edwards and Her Life After

Patti LaBelle, the Godmother of Soul, truly needs no introduction.

One of the best pieces of news we’ve heard in a long time came back in September, when Hallmark revealed that Patti, 74, would be starring in one of its record-breaking lineup of Christmas movies, Christmas Everlasting. The film, based on Marie Bostwick's novel, The Second Sister, centers on a big-city lawyer (Tatyana Ali) who is forced to return to her small hometown after her sister passes away. Thanks to the terms of her sister's eccentric will, she has to stay for at least 30 days in order to inherit the family's home, and during that time she's encouraged to make the most of the holiday season, and reevaluate her priorities.

To celebrate Patti's role in Christmas Everlasting, here's everything you need to know about her real-life romantic history.

She proposed to her husband of more than 30 years.

Getty Images

Before Armstead Edwards was Patti's husband, he was her manager. Patti shared during Oprah's Master Class, as reported by Huffington Post, that after she and Armstead fell in love, he proposed to her several times—but she turned him down. Then, she popped the question. “[He] asked me to marry him three times, and I said no," she revealed. "The fourth time, I asked him. He said, ‘Let me give it a few days.’ So he gave it a few days, and he said yes.”

The couple married in Maryland in 1969, at the Justice of the Peace. Per Patti, the reception was small and casual, featuring beer and soft-shell crab. Four years later, Patti gave birth to their first son, Zuri, and the couple went on to adopt two more children: Stanley Stocker-Edwards and Dodd Stocker-Edwards. (Fun fact: Stanley once dated Michelle Obama!)

Patti and Armstead divorced amicably in 2003.

After more than three decades of marriage, Patti said that she and Armstead came to a jarring realization. “We realized that we couldn’t live together anymore. We liked each other from a distance,” Patti said on Oprah's Master Class. “When you feel that, somebody gotta go.”

She added that things stayed incredibly amicable even as the marriage was failing. "I never hated him. He never hated me, We just couldn’t live together—not because of physical fights or anything. We never fought, thank God, in 32 years. We got along even when we didn’t get along! But then we realized that we had to leave each other," she said. In 2000, the couple announced their separation, and the divorce was finalized in 2003—and their post-split relationship is pretty aspirational. “We’re cool now,” Patti has said. “He’s one of my best friends."

And the separation hasn't slowed her down at all.

Paul Morigi

Reports emerged in 2016 that Patti had been quietly dating her 41-year-old drummer, Eric Seats, for more than a year. Eric has an extensive musical career of his own, having toured with Stevie Wonder, Mary J. Blige, and Missy Elliott, among others, and produced songs for Destiny's Child and Aaliyah.

But besides her alleged new romance, what's really been bustling is her career and family life. She's written 5 cookbooks and has her own Cooking Channel series, Patti LaBelle's Place. Her true passion, though, is her granddaughters, Gia and Leyla. "I let them make a fool of me on holidays, any day, any time they come to my house," she told People.

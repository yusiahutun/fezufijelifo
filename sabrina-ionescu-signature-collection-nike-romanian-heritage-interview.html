Sabrina Ionescu Debuts Nike Collection Inspired by Her Romanian Heritage

Nike’s newest signature athlete, WNBA star Sabrina Ionescu, has debuted a meaningful new apparel and footwear collection inspired by her heritage and made to fit everybody.

Her Sabrina 1 sneaker in the colorway Spark features a yellow-to-purple ombré transgression symbolizing the fire the basketball player brings to the court, and the subtle embroidery throughout acts as a tribute to her Romanian background. But that’s just one of many Easter eggs Ionescu included in the unisex drop.

“From day one, she was really clear that she wanted to own the signature moment,” Deepa Ramprasad, product director at Nike, tells BAZAAR.com of Ionescu’s milestone. “But of equal importance, and oftentimes of more importance to her, was recognizing two things: her family and the reality of their sacrifices that created this opportunity for her, both in sport and with Nike in this collection.”

Courtesy of Nike

Around the time of the 1989 Romanian Revolution, Ionescu’s father, Dan Ionescu, was forced to flee Communist Romania, leaving his loved ones—including the athlete’s mother, Liliana Blaj, and older brother—behind and seeking political asylum in the United States. They joined him six years later, and in 1997, Ionescu and her twin brother were born in Walnut Creek, California.

The American Romanian athlete—who played college basketball for the Oregon Ducks (a two-hour drive from Nike’s Portland headquarters) and is now with the New York Liberty—doesn’t take her family’s journey for granted. And now that she’s being recognized for her own efforts, she wants to inspire others with her story and encourage them to tell their own.

“Whatever story you have to tell, this is a shoe made for you,” Ionescu says.

Courtesy of Nike

Courtesy of Nike

A major highlight of her Sabrina 1 shoe—which comes in various colorways—is the lowercase i that wraps around the back, recognizing how Ionescu writes her last name, in accordance with Romanian custom.

Inside the shoe, Nike’s signature swoosh faces upward, symbolizing how women athletes like Ionescu are breaking the glass ceilings in the sports world. Then, inside the arch, there are the words anyone and anywhere, mirroring the basketball player’s mission to make her first collection as inclusive as possible. The flap is embellished with her new S logo for the activewear brand—a major pinch-me moment Ionescu still can’t get over.

“My favorite part is probably just seeing my logo,” she says. “You know, it’s been three years now in the making, and so finally being able to put a sweatshirt on or see the S and understand, like, that’s me, and I’m not wearing anyone else’s design, is probably the best part of it all.”

The innovation of the signature sneaker doesn’t end with the meaningful design choices. Ramprasad from Nike says that when making a basketball shoe, they tend to choose between total comfort and speed, but with this one, they had to embrace both.

“[Ionescu] said, you know, ‘I need you to help me get faster, but I need you to do that without compromising fatigue in the fourth quarter,’” Ramprasad says. “Oftentimes, we focus on one or the other—speed or comfort—but she essentially asked us to blend the two worlds. And so, that’s what built the technology inside the Sabrina 1.”

Courtesy of Nike

Courtesy of Nike

Apart from the shoe, Ionescu’s new line includes a hoodie, several T-shirts, a couple pairs of basketball shorts, and some cool socks.

“I mean, I’m a big sweatshirt person, so I think the sweatshirt is probably my favorite,” the athlete says. “I wanted to be able to style it all on and off the court. You can wear the T-shirt with jeans or with leggings, you can wear it with basketball shorts, you can kind of dress it up and down. I’d love to wear the shoe with sweats.”

What’s more, every piece is unisex—something Ionescu was adamant about from the start.

“When I grew up, I had two brothers, and they didn’t view me as a woman’s basketball player; they just, you know, viewed me as a basketball player,” she says. “And so, I wanted everyone to not really be tied down or caged into what society or what people view them as.”

Courtesy of Nike

Having said that, she is proud to be a professional woman athlete, and she hopes she is one of many female basketball players who make it to Nike’s signature league.

“Being able to represent Nike, represent myself, and have a lot of young girls, even young boys be able to see the representation is so important,” Ionescu says. “And hopefully that just continues to create equality for women in sports and to move the needle with starting girls young and just giving them this belief that they can accomplish whatever it is they want.”

With the introduction of her collection, Ionescu joins the ranks of fellow Nike signature athletes Serena Williams, Megan Rapinoe, and Naomi Osaka.

The line will release this summer at select retailers and online at nike.com.

3 things to know about ceramide capsules

If you’re looking for a way to enhance your skincare routine while reducing the number of products you use, ceramide capsules could be the answer. Made with active ingredients that help to restore the natural ceramides in your skin, these single-use capsules deliver big results in tiny micro doses.

To find out more about why capsules including powerhouse ingredients like ceramides are shaking up the beauty industry, we spoke to Dr Fiona Worsnop, consultant dermatologist at Stratum Clinics.

1. They reinforce the skin’s barrier function

Ceramides are essentially lipids (fats) that sit on the top layer of the skin, helping to support its structure and natural processes. The bad news is that the skin’s natural production of ceramides decreases as we age, weakening the skin barrier and leaving it more prone to sensitivity, dryness and the signs of ageing. This is where ceramide capsules can make a big difference.

“Ceramide capsules have the intended benefit of acting like the ceramides naturally found in the skin, helping to reinforce the barrier function,” says Dr Worsnop. “They are also particularly helpful in improving hydration in dry, sensitive skin, and in supporting the skin where natural ceramides have been depleted, which happens over time as we age.”

Pairing ceramides with additional hydrating ingredients – such as hyaluronic acid – in one potent capsule can provide powerful benefits. Elizabeth Arden’s Hyaluronic Acid Ceramide Capsules Hydra Plumping Serum, for example, draws in moisture with hyaluronic acid while ceramides help to lock in hydration. To target individual skin concerns, the beauty power house recommends applying the capsules twice a day in combination with others in the range. So the Hyaluronic Acid Ceramide Capsules can be applied morning and night alongside the Advanced Ceramide Capsules Daily Youth Restoring Serum. The result? A serum duo that hydrates and firms the skin.

2. They keep active ingredients stable for longer

Getty Images

Ceramide capsules deliver exactly the right dose with each application, helping you to cut down on wastage while receiving the optimum level of the capsule’s hero ingredient. Elizabeth Arden's capsules are made with a vegetable-based biodegradable shell and allow for full coverage of the face, neck and décolleté. The capsules contain moisture-boosting, skin-identical ceramides and these shells help to keep the ingredients inside safe from the effects of air and sunlight, which can quickly degrade actives such as vitamin C.

“Capsules are sometimes favoured as a delivery system as they’re single use, which helps to guide the appropriate amount,” says Dr Worsnop. “They’re also contained, so have the added benefit of stabilising a product that could otherwise oxidise or change when exposed to air.”

3. They help to improve the skin’s tolerance of active ingredients

If you’re hoping to introduce active ingredients into your regime but have previously found that they cause skin sensitivity, ceramide capsules can help ease you in. How so? Ceramides balance out the more potent nature of ingredients such as vitamin C and retinol by hydrating and soothing the skin. Think of it like the yin and yang of skincare pairings.

“Retinol is well known to be anti-ageing: combating the signs of sun damage, related fine lines, wrinkles and pigmentation,” explains Dr Worsnop. “Additionally, vitamin C can help to protect against the skin ageing caused by exposure to UV radiation, while increasing the amount of collagen that is produced by the skin and reducing collagen breakdown caused by UV.”

“The downside to these ingredients is that they can be aggravating to the skin, sometimes causing redness or irritation. Combining them with a hydrating ingredient such as ceramides can therefore improve the tolerance of active ingredients and help to promote hydration of the skin.”

Its Vitamin C Ceramide Capsules Radiance Renewal Serum works in much the same way, brightening the skin with antioxidants while simultaneously preventing moisture loss with ceramides.

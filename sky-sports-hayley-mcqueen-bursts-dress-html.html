Sky Sports' Hayley McQueen almost bursts out of dress

She is a favourite with sports fans offering running commentary on the latest rugby, football and tennis games as a presenter on Sky Sports.

And Hayley McQueen certainly gained some new fans as she showed off some eye-popping cleavage in a low cut sequin gown at the BT Sport Industry Awards 2017 in London on Thursday night. 

The 37-year-old was practically bursting out of the figure hugging gold dress as she posed for photographs on the red carpet.

Hayley looked every inch the television star as she showed off her buxom figure in the glamorous, sparkling gown. 

The dress featured a tight bodice with delicate spaghetti shoulder straps and a hip-hugging skirt.

Golden girl: Hayley flaunted her tanned, athletic figure in the sparkling dress which featured sequins that followed the contours of her curves 

The nude underlay was embellished with gold sequins which followed the contours of Hayley's curves. 

The Manchester-born stunner flaunted a deep glowing tan which perfectly complemented her gold dress. 

Her honey-coloured tresses were teased into delicate ringlets which fell elegantly over her shoulders. 

She accessorised her ensemble with a gold box clutch and delicate dangling diamond earrings. 

The presenter opted for a subtle make up look with a flick of black eyeliner to accentuate her blue eyes, a dusting of bronzer and blush and a slick of nude lipstick. 

Just as good from the rear: The dress showed off Hayley's pert posterior and flawless back 

Hayley is the daughter of former Manchester United and Scotland footballer Gordon McQueen. 

Last year she opened up about the pain of watching him suffer from cancer of the larynx; he is now in remission.

She told the Daily Record: 'The worst part for dad was losing the ability to voice his opinion. He’s so passionate about football and loved doing commentary for Sky Sports and meeting ex-professionals.'

Star guests: Other attendees at the sports bash included Princess Beatrice and Tom Daley

She also revealed that she inherited her love of sport from Gordon. 

'He’s given me an understanding of the game that I probably wouldn’t have otherwise.

'Having been around football from a young age, I’m knowledgeable about the game and sometimes that surprises people.'

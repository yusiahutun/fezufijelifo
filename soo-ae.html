Professional Life and Career

Soo Ae was on the verge of joining a K-pop star group. A record representative approached the young hottie on the street in the upscale Apgujeong neighborhood, just out of high school. She engaged through six months of arduous work but ended up with no record to release. After acting in the 2004 successful historical drama Emperor of the Sea, which was transferred to various Asian nations, South America, and the Middle East, presenting her to a broader worldwide audience, she rocketed to stardom.

Soo Ae earned the title “queen of tears” for her well-received roles in melodramas, thanks to her classical beauty and innocent, graceful image. In the 2007 romantic comedy Two Outs in the Ninth Inning, she played a 30-year-old foul-mouthed, unkempt, and cynical single lady battling with life and love, alongside Lee Jung-jin.

Soo Ae featured with Jung Jae-young in the comedy Wedding Campaign and Lee Byung-hun in the melodrama Once in the Summer after a triumphant big-screen debut in A Family. Sunny, a film about an average housewife who becomes a “consolatory band” singer to find her husband, who has been assigned to fight alongside American forces in the Vietnam War, was released in 2008.

More Details

Soo Ae appeared in the 2010 suspense thriller Midnight FM after being dissatisfied with melodrama screenplays and wanting to try parts in new genres. Soo Ae returned to television in Athena: Goddess of War, playing a cold-blooded double agent who underwent martial arts training to execute her dramatic action scenes in the espionage series.

Moving on, Soo quit Star J Entertainment, her 12-year agency, in September 2013 to join Management Soop. She was subsequently cast in Taking Off 2, the sequel to the 2009 smash sports drama, as a North Korean defector who joins the first South Korean women’s national ice hockey team. She appeared in KBS2’s romantic comedy Sweet Stranger and Me in 2016, nine years after her last romantic comedy-drama Two Outs in the Ninth Inning. Likewise, She appeared in the drama film High Society in 2018. She signed with Huayi Brothers, a news agency, in May 2019.

Teen nearly dies after taking popular weight-loss pills

Taylor Hannah turned to herbal diet pills in a bid to lose weight – but the products nearly killed her.

The 19-year-old ballooned from 10st to 14st after suffering a side effect from her bipolar medication.

Last August, she decided to purchase green tea extracts and fat metabolisers from Holland & Barrett so she could get back into shape.

Taylor popped the pills for six months until she had to be rushed to casualty and put on a drip because of severe dehydration.

Doctors revealed that the tablets had ripped her stomach lining and caused chronic vomiting.

Taylor said: "I almost died from dehydration and needed to be put on a fluid drip due to excessive vomiting. I was really ill, couldn't move and had chest pains.

"I have now found out they have torn my stomach lining."

The petite 5ft 3in teen lost almost half of her body weight since taking the pills and being unwell, dropping to a tiny 7st 9lb.

She was hospitalised in February and the teenager still struggles with eating.

Taylor said: "I thought I was going to die. I was constantly being sick and if I hadn't gone to hospital when I did, I would have died because I was so dehydrated.

"No amount of water would fix it. I was drinking up to six or seven litres of water a day and nothing helped.

"Doctors told me the caffeine from the diet pills could have caused the dehydration."

Taylor had taken one Nature's Garden green tea extract tablet per day in line with the product’s recommendations.

The pills, which cost £19.99 for 100, claim to boost metabolism and assist with weight loss.

But she later found out that one pill is the equivalent of five cups of green tea, meaning she swallowed a whopping 420 cups of green tea before switching to Nutrition HQ's fat metabolisers in November.

The red metabolism capsules, which cost £12.99, claim to promote healthy weight loss by burning fat into muscle.

She said: "I researched diet pills loads before I actually bought them.

"Then when I went into the shop the girl recommended these two in particular, telling me they were the best."

But now Taylor believes the pills made her unwell.

She added: "The GP said it could possibly be the diet pill and another doctor said he has also had reports that they can rip your stomach lining."

The young woman from Inverclyde is still in chronic pain and can only eat yoghurt and drink milk. Anything else can cause severe vomiting and makes her stomach swell.

Taylor said: "I'm struggling to eat without vomiting and sometimes I can't even drink water. I get a burning sensation and then I'm sick and then get severe stomach cramps.

"It's slowly getting better but I'm still not sleeping because I'm in so much pain.

"The cramps keep me awake at night, it's horrible because I'm so hungry but I can't eat anything because it hurts too much."

She said she feels angry with herself for ignoring friends’ warnings and buying in to the diet pill fad.

She said: "I feel ridiculous for taking them now. Everybody told me not to.

"I fell into the trap of feeling pressured to look a particular way, and took a short cut to achieve it. I regret ever taking the pills.

"I want people to know that just because pills may be herbal doesn't mean they are safe.

"These pills are still being sold to teenagers as young as 16 and I don't want anyone at any age to go through what I am going through."

A spokesperson for Holland & Barrett told Daily Star Online: "All our products are manufactured to the highest standards, used by tens of thousands of people every day.

"We have never received a complaint of this kind from these products before.

"Certain products we sell, including weight management supplements, do carry an on-pack warning instructing customers to consult their GP if, for example, they are already taking prescribed medicines.

"Clearly we are concerned to hear of any customer reportedly becoming unwell, and we would always urge customers to seek advice from our team of nutritionists or trained store staff."

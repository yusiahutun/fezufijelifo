The Obsession of K-pop Culture in India and its Psychological Impacts

South Korea is a nation with a population of slightly more than 51 million in East Asia, with more than half of its citizens living in Seoul City, its capital. The nation is prone to cyclones, tidal waves, and flood tides because of its position in the southern part of the Korean foreland. even so, this doesn’t obviate the nation from being one of the most developed in the world and from exercising significant influence across several areas.

Currently, Korean entertainment has devoted followers around the world, and this was not by chance. The Korean government recognized the significance of artistic supply and has invested millions in government enterprise and allocations to spread a specific strain of Korean culture around the world for mass consumption.
This article will shed some light on the obsession with K-pop culture and its psychological impacts on its fans.

K-pop Wave in India:

The South Korean music and entertainment manner known as “K-pop” is distinguished by its engaging songs, mind-blowing illustrations, and affectionate fandoms. Along with introducing a new type of entertainment to the Indian following, this artistic surge also gave rise to a rising obsession with K-pop.
For the past several years, Gen-Z and millennials in India have been heavily told by K-pop and K-dramas. They’re part of the Hallyu wave of Korean culture, which includes other exports from the nation like K-beauty and Korean cookery.

The recent pandemic appears to have given K-pop and K-drama its biggest break in India, though K-beauty is still fairly popular among Indians. further people had the time and inclination to experiment with varied forms of content until they set up commodities that delivered them with the ideal way to relax from the anxiety of the times.

Korean drama, Korean music, and variety shows make up the majority of the country’s entertainment industry. Stars from all three stripes blend interchangeably. K-pop bands like BTS, EXO & Blackpink are the world-known faces of K-pop culture.

Over several months now, India has risen to the top five or six nations in the world in terms of views for K-pop artists like Blackpink and BTS’s music tapes on YouTube. The Internet and the simple availability of English-subtitled interpretations of all popular songs and dramas ensure this kind of obsession.

Positive Psychological Impacts:
  • Emotional Satisfaction: K-pop gives fans a way to express their passions. Love, fellowship, and individual development are common themes in K-pop songwriting and music, which can connect with listeners and offer catharsis in an emotional position.
  • Sense of Connection: Partaking in K-pop audiences fosters a feeling of community and belonging. Fans who are passionate about K-pop constantly perceive a connection to one another.
  • Alleviation & Boost: A lot of K-pop stars are presented as extremely blessed, disciplined, and active people. Their beloveds see them as role models who encourage them to put in additional effort, follow their feelings and brush their proficiency.
  • Cultural Expression: The cultural expression of K-pop is inspiring. Fans can express their creativity through their K-pop charisma by taking part in audience-related activities like dance covers, fan art, fan fiction and learning Korean.
  • Cultural Awareness: Being exposed to K-pop encourages curiosity about and a sense of artistic mindfulness for South Korean culture. Fans embrace artistic diversity and widen their horizons by learning about the Korean language, traditions, and customs.
Negative Psychological Impacts:
  • Over-identification with icons: A dangerous over-identification with K-pop icons may come in some fans. This may lead to an unhealthy attachment to the lives and well-being of these stars as well as a hazy sense of who they are.
  • Distraction and Avoidance of Reality: Although K-pop provides a way to escape from everyday life, some fans may grow overly dependent on this escape, avoiding problems and liabilities in real life.
  • Fan Wars and Cyberbullying: Fan wars, cyberbullying, and aggravation can sometimes turn online fan communities into toxic surroundings. Those who witness or share similar behaviours may face mischievous psychological impacts from this.
  • Unrealistic Beauty norms: The flaring and well-readied pictures of K-pop icons may immortalize unattainable norms of beauty. Fans, especially the adolescent generation, could witness pressure to live up to these prospects, which could affect problems with self-regard and body image.
  • High pressure to Conform: Clinging to K-pop trends and fandom culture may sometimes cause someone to lose their oneness and authenticity. Fans may witness psychological damage if they feel pressured to live up to the norms of the fan base.
Strategies to Address these Impacts:
  • Encouraging Mindfulness: Motivate fans to balance their interests in K-pop with their liabilities in real life by being self-conscious.
  • Media knowledge and critical thinking: Instruct fans in the critical analysis of media material, encompassing the life and beauty ideals presented in K-pop to develop a more realistic approach.
  • Healthy surroundings: Encourage an atmosphere of decency and respect in online fan networks to foster healthy online engagement. To keep online surroundings safe and encouraging, it’s important to address toxic gist and cyberbullying.
  • Different Interests: Motivate followers to continue pursuing a variety of pursuits and interests. This promotes a well-rounded sense of self and hinders an over-identification with K-pop.
  • Professional Assistance: Seeking remedy or counseling from therapists may be important for people who are suffering severe psychological misery as a result of their obsession with K-pop.

Art in all its forms is exquisite. It brightens one’s day and calms the soul. K-pop is an amazing cultural medium. They developed it as a way of expressing themselves and emphasizing the beauty of Korean culture, just like all other art forms. The work of art is pure. It’s fully up to the individuals how they choose to play with what’s in their hands. In the end, K-pop will presumably always have an impact in India, therefore it’s important to encourage a healthy and balanced relationship with this caution.

Hit back: Country music star Tim McGraw has slammed a new report which claims he kept a secret son from his current wife Faith Hill

Country music star Tim McGraw has slammed a new report which claims he kept a 'secret son' from his current wife Faith Hill.

The 45-year-old has spoken out after 23-year-old Tyler Zarbo told the National Enquirer that the singer helped raise him when McGraw was engaged to his mother Kristine Donahue.

While Zarbo is not McGraw's biological child, according to the Florida student, the For A Little While star still continued a close relationship with him after he parted ways with Donahue in the mid-nineties.

'Tim McGraw was like a father to me when I was growing up,' Zarbo told the magazine.

'He saw me as the son he never had, and we've stayed in contact. I wish he could have remained my substitute dad, but it just wasn't meant to be.' 

National Enquirer reports that McGraw immediately took Zarbo under his wing when he was in a relationship with Donahue.

'Tim made a vow to Kristine that he's marry her and help raise her boy,' a source told the publication.

But after McGraw went on tour with his current wife Faith, his relationship with Donahue was quickly doomed after the two country music stars fell in love.

According to the publication, it wasn't too long before McGraw had split with Donahue and went on to marry Faith in 1996.

The country music couple now have three children, including Gracie, 15, Maggie, 14 and Audrey, 11.

But according to the insider, McGraw still stayed in touch with his former flame and her son after he was married, infuriating Hill in the process.

'After that, Tim stayed in touch with Kristine and Tyler,' the source said. 'But when Faith found out, she was furious.

Happy couple: Tim and Faith have been married for 16 years and have three daughters together

'Tim had cut all ties to Kristine and keep his contact with Tyler under the radar.'

On Wednesday McGraw's representative Mary Hillard denied several claims in the National Enquirer story.

'The National Enquirer story is full of inaccuracies and misrepresentations about Tim and Faith,' she told People magazine.

'We aren't going to comment on false statements that appear to have been sensationalized for the purpose of selling tabloid magazines.'

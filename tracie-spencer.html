Biography

Tracie Spencer was born in Waterloo, IA on July 12, 1976. Known best for her chart-topping 1991 hit “Tender Kisses,” this rhythm & blues vocalist and songwriter also released numerous other popular singles, including “Save Your Love,” “This House,” and “Love Me.” During a guest appearance on the popular ABC series Family Matters, she performed a duet version of her hit song “Tender Kisses” with series star Darius McCrary. After winning a 1980s edition of Star Search with her rendition of Whitney Houston’s “How Will I Know,” she was offered a Capitol Records contract. By the age of eleven, she had recorded her first album on the label.

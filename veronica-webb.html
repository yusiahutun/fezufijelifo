Veronica Webb is a popular American Model, Actress, Writer, and Television Personality. Veronica Webb is widely known as the first African-American to win a contract with a major cosmetics firm.

Early Life and Childhood

Veronica Webb was born on February 25, 1965, in the city of Detroit, Michigan, United States. Her age is 58 years as of 2023 and her full name is also Veronica Webb.

Similarly, she is American by nationality and practices Christianity by religion. However, she belongs to the African-American ethnicity and her zodiac sign is Pisces by date of birth.

Talking about her family members, her father’s name is Leonard Douglas Webb who was an electrician and her mother’s name is Marion Stewart who is a retired Army lieutenant captain. Likewise, one of her sisters is an oncologist and another is a mathematician however, their names are not available.

Veronica Webb – Education

In terms of her educational background, she has done her schooling at Detroit Waldorf School in Detroit, Michigan.

She graduated in 1983 and moved to New York City where she studied design. However, she dropped out of school to pursue her modelling career at an early age.

Career and Professional Life

Moving on to Webb’s career, she dropped out of school after a makeup artist noticed her in a housewares store. She also appeared in the music videos for New Order’s Round and Round from their 1989 album Technique, as well as Scritti Politti’s Perfect Way and Wood Beez from their 1985 album Cupid & Psyche 85.

Similarly, she pursued a modelling career in New York, eventually becoming a Revlon spokesmodel. She was the first black supermodel to sign an exclusive contract with a major cosmetics firm. Also, she made her film debut in Spike Lee’s Jungle Fever in 1991.

Likewise, her other films include Someone Like You, The Big Tease, and Malcolm X. She also had recurring roles on television series such as Damon, Just Shoot Me!, Becker, and Clueless. Also, she starred in the independent feature film Dirty Laundry in 2006. She modelled for Revlon, Victoria’s Secret, Azzedine Alaia, Karl Lagerfeld, Isaac Mizrahi, and Todd Oldham.

Furthermore, she has written essays and columns for magazines such as Interview, Paper, Details, Panorama, Elle, The New York Times Syndicate, The Sunday Times, Conde Nast’s Cookie, and Esquire. Miramax Books released Veronica Webb Sight: Adventures In The Big City, a collection of autobiographical essays, in 1998.

Additionally, while she was in line at the White House, Harvey Weinstein, chairman of Miramax, approached her about publishing her writings as a memoir. She co-hosted the inaugural season of Tim Gunn’s Guide To Style on Bravo. Also, she was named to the Eucerin Skin First Council in 2010 to promote skin health.
